## Design Context

### Users
Students aged 18–25 searching for internships and first jobs in France. They are comfortable with modern tools (Notion, Figma, Linear) but get overwhelmed quickly. They need to feel *capable and in control*, not buried under information. They use this daily — it should feel like a focused work tool, not a platform.

### Brand Personality
Determined · Credible · Forward-looking.  
The interface should feel like a premium planning tool: focused, calm, professional. Not a flashy startup, not a corporate job board. Think "studio" — quiet confidence, every element earns its place.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Fonts**: Epilogue (display/headings) + Lexend (body) — both chosen for clarity and distinctiveness, neither in the AI-default reject list
- **Palette**: Forest green brand (`oklch(44% 0.13 158)`) on warm off-white (`oklch(97.5% 0.008 85)`) background. Avoids the overused teal and indigo of the previous iteration.
- **Theme**: Light mode only. Students use this in daytime, in libraries, cafes — a warm, well-lit surface suits the context.
- **Sidebar navigation**: App feels more like a focused workspace than a website
- **Anti-references**: No hero metrics with big numbers everywhere. No left-border stripe accents. No gradient text. No card-within-card nesting. No purple-to-blue gradients. No emoji decorations in copy.

### Design Principles
1. **One thing at a time.** Each screen has one primary job. Secondary information is available, not front-and-center.
2. **Lists beat grids.** For job results and applications, clean rows with good typography outperform card grids.
3. **Trust through density control.** Don't show everything at once. Progressive disclosure keeps students focused.
4. **Actions close to content.** Save, Generate, Apply — the most important buttons sit right next to the content they act on.
5. **Encourage, don't judge.** Copy should feel like a knowledgeable friend, not a corporate product.
